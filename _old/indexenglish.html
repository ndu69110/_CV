[ENSIBS] Engineer Project

Étude du Deepfake

Every year in my engineering school, we had to carry out a “major” project. The major 1st year project is carrying out a scientific study on a subject of our choice related to cybersecurity.

The goal of this project was to introduce us the scientifical research world by teaching us how to find interesting articles for our subject in research databases (IEE / arxiv / ACM / Springer ...), how to cross-reference scientific articles (google scholar / zotero ...) and how to write a scientific article with the LaTeX language.

For this project I decided to write a scientific article about the different methods of creating and detecting Deepfake.

The students' articles who obtained the maximum score were submitted for peer review by real researchers (to be potentially published in a scientific journal). Several students obtained the maximum mark, this was also my case, unfortunately no students were published this year.

OffGrid is a decentralized platform based on the Volweb tool for setting up Bash Bunny keys for doing dump RAM.

The idea is to allow any technician to perform an extraction of the RAM of a corrupted machine without waiting for a forensic expert.

OffGrid also makes it possible to analyze the recovered RAM and to generate a signed forensic report admissible by justice.

Offgrid could also contain other configuration profiles such as system/network administration or hardening tasks (these profiles have not been written).

Locked Shields 2024
Previous Next

Locked Shields is the world's largest cybersecurity exercise. Each year, it is organized to train NATO teams, 40 different nations bringing together more than 3000 experts compete for 4 days.

For my final year engineering school project I had the chance to participate in the organization of this exercise, in collaboration with the University of Tallinn (Estonia) and the CCDCOE (The NATO Cooperative Cyber Defence Centre of Excellence).

We were part of the Green Team (development team), our role was to implemented a WebSSO solution used during the exercise scenario. This solution had to meet very specific constraints to interact with the global infrastructure (in particular being deployable with Ansible).

Team members visited the site before and during the exercise to ensure the availability of the delivered solution.

JBox is a Node.js application designed for owners of establishments (bars, restaurants, bowling, etc.) wanting to create a musical atmosphere personalized by customers. This project was the last carried out as part of my studies in University Diploma in Computer Science at IUT Charlemagne.

IDSniffer

Results examples

Previous Next

IDSniffer is a Python script allowing you to list all files containing plaintext identifiers from a root folder.

I wrote this Python script shortly after being recruited as an apprentice cybersecurity engineer at GHBS. Indeed, I noticed the presence of numerous files containing clear identifiers in the shared folders of the company.

The script lists files containing character strings referring to identifiers (example: "password = " / "creds are" / "login:" ...)
The script is able to read files with the following extensions:
["csv", "docx", "doc", "gif", "jpg", "json", "html", "msg ", "odt", "pdf", "png", "pptx", "txt", "xlsx", "xls"]

Human verification is however necessary because the script returns many false positives, no process to ignore these false positives has yet been implemented.

Previous Next

Ryoshi is a Python program allowing you to send personalized Phishing emails via a graphical interface, while bypassing the SPF / DKIM / DMARC protocols.

The objective of the project was to deepen my knowledge of email security, in fact I carried out this project at the same time as the implementation of a DMARC policy in my company.

Ryoshi allows you to check if an email exists (thanks to Holehe), it also allows you to create/personalize your own phishing email and of course to send it with a fake email address.

Ryoshi means "fisherman" in Japanese, it is greatly inspired by the espoofer tool presented at the conference BlackHat USA 2020.
